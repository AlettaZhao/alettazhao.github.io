In Defence of Useless Knowledge

28 February 2026
reflection

Whenever someone asks what my studies are good for, I think of the tree in Zhuangzi. It survived precisely because it was useless to the carpenter. He walked past, glanced at it, shook his head. The tree lived for centuries.

Modern society worships utility with something close to obsession. Every discipline must answer: can it be monetised? What is the return on investment? The logic is not wrong — if you treat life as an optimisation problem.

But life is not an optimisation problem. Or if it is, we have not yet defined the objective function.

On Leisure

Aristotle held that leisure was the foundation of civilisation. The word school descends from the Greek scholē, meaning leisure. Schools were supposed to be places for unhurried thought.

Real learning needs blank space. Like the flying white in calligraphy, the negative space in a painting, the rest in music. Without the useless gaps, the useful parts cannot stand.
