A Winter Afternoon in the Café

15 January 2026
life

The sky outside is the colour of lead. The heating has fogged up the glass. Someone at the next table draws a sun on it with a fingertip. This is probably the closest the day will come to clear skies.

A middle-aged man in the corner has a thick book open in front of him, but has not turned a page in twenty minutes. His gaze passes over the words and lands somewhere I cannot see. Perhaps he is thinking of a person. Perhaps he is simply staring. Staring is an underrated art.

On Cold Coffee

An americano left long enough will go cold. This is the second law of thermodynamics in its everyday form. But cold coffee lets you taste more layers — the acidity, the fruit notes, even a trace of woody sweetness that heat had concealed.

Perhaps many things work this way. In the heat of the moment, perception is coarse. Only when things cool down do the details surface.

Half past five. It is already dark. Stepping outside, the cold wind arrives like a brief greeting.
