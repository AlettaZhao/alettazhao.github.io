Time Is a Snake Biting Its Own Tail

12 March 2026
reading

Márquez lays out a peculiar notion of time in the rise and fall of the Buendía family. It is not linear, not simply cyclical, but something closer to a Möbius strip — you think you have arrived somewhere new, then look down and find the footprints are your own.

All moments exist simultaneously.

A Genealogy of Solitude

The solitude in the title is not a mood. It is a condition of existence. Every Buendía is sealed inside a private world. Between them there is blood, love, hatred — but never true understanding.
